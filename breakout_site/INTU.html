Investment thesis
Thesis. Intuit fell ~67% from its 52-week high to near a multi-year low ($267 vs $813 high), yet it still beat Q3 FY26 EPS ($12.80 vs $12.57, +1.8%), carries 33 buys vs 4 sells, a $452.94 consensus target (~70% implied upside), 14.9% ROE and rising forward EPS. The bull case is a classic overshoot in a high-quality compounder (QuickBooks ecosystem + Credit Karma) trading at a depressed ~13x forward EPS. The crucial caveat: the sell-off is NOT pure sentiment - the May earnings call confirmed the TurboTax DIY/Consumer business is losing share at the low end with the DIY tax segment estimated declining ~14-15%, and management was directly pressed on GenAI changing the competitive landscape. That is a partial structural threat, not a clean overreaction.
Key risk. The Consumer/TurboTax decline is at least partly secular: free GenAI tax/accounting agents could permanently compress the DIY funnel (a 2M-unit drop in free filers was flagged), and consensus targets may lag if estimates are cut further. This is a value-with-a-falling-knife setup, not a clean V-bottom.

Investment thesis
Thesis. Adobe is down ~50% from its high to $195 (just above its 52-week low) and trades at roughly 7-8x forward EPS - an extraordinarily compressed multiple for a franchise still beating estimates (Q2 FY26 EPS $5.96 vs $5.82, +2.4%; revenue beat 2.5%), growing ~11%, with ~25% margins and rising forward EPS. The bull case is a deep-value, heavy-FCF moat (Creative Cloud + Acrobat + Experience Cloud) that could spring-load on any sentiment turn. But this is genuinely mixed: the latest leg down was self-inflicted/structural-leaning - Adobe deliberately cut its FY26 organic ARR outlook by ~$500M and dropped its organic ARR growth target, deferring Creative Cloud price increases to chase freemium AI users (organic ARR growth now ~8%), and lost BOTH its CEO (transition announced) and CFO (Dan Durn to Marvell). Multiple firms downgraded post-earnings (Evercore to In Line PT $225; Stifel; Jefferies/Boursorama Hold PT $230).
Key risk. AI monetization is unanswered and the leadership vacuum (new CEO + interim CFO) means sentiment may not turn until both seats are filled, per Evercore. The freemium pivot is a real revenue-growth haircut, not just optics - if generative-AI rivals (OpenAI, Google, Canva, Midjourney) keep pressuring the funnel, the 'value trap' case is live.

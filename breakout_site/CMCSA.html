Investment thesis
Thesis. Comcast sits at essentially a multi-year low ($22.43, 0.4% of its 52-week range) and screens optically cheap (~6x forward EPS, 40% implied upside to a $31.48 consensus, beat Q1 by 9%). But this is the clearest falling-knife in the basket and the decline is justified/structural, not an overreaction: the core broadband business is in secular decline (181k subs lost in Q4 - a steeper drop YoY; full-year 2026 loss guided ~590k), ARPU is now FALLING (-3.1%), and the company is buying retention with free-wireless-line giveaways and a 5-year price guarantee that further pressures ARPU and EBITDA (Connectivity EBITDA -4% YoY). New existential threats are emerging (Rosenblatt cut to $24 citing Starlink/SpaceX broadband competition; fixed-wireless from T-Mobile). Forward EPS is essentially flat-to-down, not rising. A shareholder at the June AGM called it a company that 'requires repositioning' - the $30B NBCU networks now valued ~$5B post-Versant spinoff, and Comcast lost both the Warner Bros and the bid contests.
Key risk. If broadband losses do not stabilize and Starlink/fixed-wireless accelerate, the cheap multiple is a value trap and 'cheap gets cheaper'; the 4.5%+ dividend is the main support, but a low-growth cable utility in secular share loss does not fit a pre-explosion breakout fingerprint. This is a yield/defensive name, not a turnaround spring.

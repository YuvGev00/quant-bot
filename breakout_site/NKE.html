Investment thesis
Thesis. Nike is down ~44% from its high to $45 near a multi-year low, but the setup is a self-inflicted, management-driven turnaround rather than a secular collapse - and the most recent print is the strongest positive surprise in the basket (Q3 FY26 EPS $0.35 vs $0.29 estimated, +20.4%). CEO Elliott Hill is executing a deliberate reset: pulling back classic-franchise supply, repositioning Nike Digital as full-price, liquidating inventory via markdowns (the cause of the margin trough), and reinvesting in wholesale and demand creation. North America has made the most progress; gross margin already troughed (Q1 FY26 down 320bps to 42.2% on clearance + tariffs) and forward EPS shows a clear FY27 recovery shape. With 37 buys vs 5 sells, a $62 consensus (~37% upside) and the strongest brand in apparel, this looks like a recovery off a managed trough, not a broken business.
Key risk. The turnaround is back-half-weighted and geographically uneven: Greater China (declining store traffic, heavy promotion, channel inventory) and Converse remain drags expected to persist into FY27, and trough ROE (~3.7%) plus tariff cost pressure mean a slower-than-hoped margin recovery would re-rate the stock down. It is a multi-quarter execution bet on Hill's reset taking hold.
